Courses

Elementary Courses (6 - 9)

Basic level courses (6 to 9 years) facilitate the gradual integration of students into the IRA's learning environment through nurturing three key competencies: autonomy, communication and creativity. Using classic Lego bricks, plug-in electronics and user-friendly block-based programming software, students are given time to experiment and explore topics in mathematics, mechanics, design, programming and solving problems. Junior Courses (10 - 13)

Junior courses (10 to 13 years) offer a mix of basic and intermediate topics from engineering, robotics, electronics, mechanics and animations. The courses are designed to introduce students to topics from engineering design, systematic problem solving, research and review of literature, hypotheses and design of experiments, as well as management over time and organizations. Senior Courses (14 - 16)

Senior courses (14 to 16 years) offer a range of project-based courses that emphasize the practical dimension engineering, where students are taught how to develop real-world solutions. Courses talk about advanced topics in microcontrollers, mobile application development, engineering solutions and image processing. At this level, students get to know each other with product engineering and design cycles and develop product prototypes by the end of each course cycle.
